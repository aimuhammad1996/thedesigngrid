About

I've spent 5+ years designing inside both regulated banks and early-stage startups — building the interface systems that decide whether users trust a product enough to stay.

From an internal treasury platform used by traders to founding-designer roles at two startups, my work sits at the intersection of complex systems and clean execution — component libraries, Figma-to-React handoff, and interfaces that hold up under real constraints, not ideal ones.

  • 01

    Comfortable in ambiguity

    I don't wait for a fully-formed brief. I help frame the problem when no one's sure what it is yet.

  • 02

    Systems over screens

    I design the model underneath the interface — states, flows, edge cases — so the product scales past version one.

  • 03

    Fluent in startup constraints

    Runway, headcount, and speed are design inputs, not excuses. I ship inside real constraints, not ideal ones.

Process

How the work actually happens.

Not a rigid ritual — a repeatable way of reducing risk before pixels get precious.

01

Discover

Stakeholder interviewsData auditUser research

I start with the business problem, not the design brief. Interviews, analytics, support tickets, and sales call recordings tell me more than a Figma file ever will.

02

Frame

Problem statementSuccess metricsConstraints map

Before any UI, I align the team on what we're actually solving, how we'll know it worked, and what real constraints — technical, regulatory, timeline — the design has to live inside.

03

Design

Flows & systemsPrototypingUI craft

I work in flows and states before screens, then bring the same rigor to typography, spacing, and motion — the details that separate "functional" from "fundable."

04

Validate

Usability testingStakeholder reviewIteration

Assumptions get tested with real users before engineering commits real time. I'd rather be wrong in a prototype than wrong in production.

05

Ship & measure

Phased rolloutInstrumentationImpact review

I stay through launch — phased rollouts, dashboards to track the metrics we set in step two, and a retro on what the system should learn next.
